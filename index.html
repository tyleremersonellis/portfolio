Experience
2021 – present
Currently, I am a civilian Naval Architect for the Navy with NAVSEA’s Future Concepts and Systems Integration Branch. I’ve worked on projects in systems integration, general arrangements, ship concept design, 3D modeling, and data management.

2023
Paper I co-authored was published in Nature science journal. In this report we studied cicada wing expansion by tracking the hydraulic processes of wing unfurling during the Brood X mass emergence in May 2021. A promising area of study for new bio-inspired microfluidic devices and soft deployable robotics.

2022
Conference presentation I gave at the 2022 Virtual Society of Integrative and Comparative Biology conference. This experiment investigates the potential passive physical requirements for successful flying snake landings using a physical model experiment. Results from this physical experiment will be further compared to theoretical chain-rope models to lend insight into the passive mechanics of capture of a thin, flexible system.

2017 – 2021
Bachelor of Science at Virginia Tech with a major in Ocean Engineering and minor in Naval Engineering.

During my undergraduate studies I joined the Human Powered Submarine design team and Dr. Jake Socha’s Biomechanics Lab. For Human Powered Submarine I gained hands-on experience in wet layups and 3D part design and fabrication. For the Biomechanics lab I worked on data collection, MATLAB image post-processing, and point-tracking of tree snake branch gap crossing behavior.

I participated in undergraduate research with Professor Christine Gilbert, where I worked as a lab volunteer for an Office of Naval Research funded wedge drop research project and worked on a semester of control surface research investigating the impacts of fin geometry on hydrodynamic performance for the Human Powered Submarine team's competition submarine.

2020
I was a Concept Research Intern at the Center for Innovation in Ship Design (CISD), where I worked on a launch and recovery future concept ship design. I 3D Modeled the ship to meet International Maritime Organization stability standards and estimated ship resistance and fuel consumption.

2018
I was a Engineering Intern at Dominion Mechanical Conctractors Inc. I reviewed mechanical and architectural drawings to identify change orders and reported findings to senior engineers.
